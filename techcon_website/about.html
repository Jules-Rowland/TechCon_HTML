TechCon has evolved into a prominent global technology conference, with various iterations focusing on different sectors and regions.One notable series is the SVC TechCon, organized by the Society of Vacuum Coaters. Established in 1957, it has been a key event for the vacuum coating industry, emphasizing thin-film deposition and surface engineering technologies. Over the decades, SVC TechCon has expanded its scope, incorporating advancements in digital transformation, data analytics, and process automation. The conference serves as a platform for researchers, engineers,and industry professionals to share insights and drive innovation in the field.

The goals and driving principles of the TechCon conference center around fostering innovation, collaboration, and thought leadership across the global technology ecosystem. Whether in sectors like AI, semiconductors, cybersecurity, or business-tech integration, TechCon is designed to:

Goals of TechCon

  1. Inspire Innovation: Provide a platform for showcasing groundbreaking technologies, research, and startups that are shaping the future.
  2. Facilitate Knowledge Exchange: Enable experts, professionals, and enthusiasts to share insights, breakthroughs, and lessons learned.
  3. Bridge Industry Gaps: Connect academia, startups, corporations, and government bodies to foster synergy and accelerate progress.
  4. Promote Skill Development: Offer workshops, panels, and exhibitions to help attendees upskill and stay ahead in their fields.
  5. Support Emerging Leaders: Empower students and young professionals by offering access to industry veterans and mentorship opportunities.
  6. Encourage Ethical Tech Use: Highlight responsible innovation, data privacy, and sustainability in tech deployment.

Driving Principles

  1. Future-Readiness: Prioritizing forward-thinking conversations that prepare individuals and organizations for technological change.
  2. Accessibility and Inclusion: Ensuring that voices from underrepresented communities and regions are heard and valued.
  3. Evidence-Based Discourse: Encouraging decisions and insights rooted in research, data, and real-world impact.
  4. Sustainability and Responsibility: Promoting tech solutions that contribute to global challenges like climate change, health, and equitable access.
  5. Collaboration Over Competition: TechCon thrives on open dialogue and partnership rather than rivalry.

Meet Our Speakers

Jack T. Kavalieros is an Intel Fellow and Vice President directing the novel device and process integration teams in Intel's Components Research. With a distinguished career at Intel, he has been instrumental in advancing semiconductor technologies. At TechCon 2022, Kavalieros delivered a compelling keynote titled "The Future of Semiconductor Innovation," where he emphasized the importance of engaging future innovators in the semiconductor industry. His insights underscored the critical role of research and development in sustaining the industry's growth and innovation.


Alex Lieberman is the Co-Founder and Executive Chairman of Morning Brew, a media company delivering business news to over 4 million subscribers. At TechCon 2023, Lieberman served as the keynote speaker, sharing his journey of transforming Morning Brew from a college project into a leading media brand. His talk provided valuable insights into entrepreneurship, media innovation, and the evolving landscape of digital content.
